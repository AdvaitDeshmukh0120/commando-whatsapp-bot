=== FEATURE  ===

Q: Which switches support stacking? Does any switch support stacking?
A: ONLY the *C3500 Series* (Marshall) supports stacking via VSF (Virtual Switch Framework). Models: C3500-24X, C3500-24X-2Q, C3500-24X-2C. No other COMMANDO switch series supports stacking. The C3500 also supports MLAG (Multi-Chassis Link Aggregation) as an alternative to stacking.

Q: Which switches support MPLS?
A: ONLY the *C3500 Series* (MarshallOS L3+) supports MPLS. Features: LDP, RSVP, FRR, 6PE, L2VPN, L3VPN, VPWS, VPLS. No other series supports MPLS.

Q: Which switches support VXLAN?
A: ONLY the *C3500 Series* (MarshallOS L3+) supports VXLAN overlay, as well as NVGRE and GENEVE tunnels.

Q: Which switches support BGP?
A: ONLY the *C3500 Series* (MarshallOS L3+) supports BGP (BGP4 for IPv4, BGP4+ for IPv6).

Q: Which switches support OSPF?
A: *C3500 Series* (MarshallOS L3+): OSPFv2 and OSPFv3. *C3000 and E3000 Series* (SoldierOS L3): OSPFv2. C2000/E2000 (SoldierOS L2+) do NOT support OSPF.

Q: Which switches support VLAN?
A: ALL managed switches support VLAN: C3500, C3000, E3000, IE3000, C2000, E2000, E1300. They support port-based VLAN, tag-based VLAN (802.1Q), Voice VLAN (on E2000/C2000), Surveillance VLAN, Protocol VLAN, QinQ. Unmanaged switches (E1100, E1000, IE1000, E300, E200, E100) do NOT support VLAN configuration.

Q: Which switches support PoE? Which switches have PoE?
A: Most COMMANDO switches have PoE variants. The "P" or "GP" in model names indicates PoE. For example: E2000-24GP-8CF (24-port GE PoE+), E1000-16GP-4CF (16-port GE PoE+), C3000-24GP-4X (24-port GE PoE+). The E100 Series does NOT have PoE. Models without "P" in the name (like E2000-8G-4CF) are non-PoE.

Q: Which switches have 10G ports?
A: *C3500 Series*: 24x10G SFP+ (core ports). *C3000 Series*: 4x/6x10G SFP+ uplinks. *E3000 Series*: 4x/6x10G SFP+ uplinks plus dedicated 10G models. *IE3000 Series*: 4x10G SFP+ uplinks. *E1100 Series*: 2x10G SFP+ uplinks. *E1000-8X*: 8x10G SFP+. C2000, E2000, E1300, E300, E200, E100 do NOT have 10G.
